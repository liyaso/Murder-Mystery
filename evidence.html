Evidence File 01

Letter Opener

A brass letter opener was found on Victor Langley’s desk with traces of blood on the blade.

Investigators believe it was taken from the desk during an argument and used in the attack.

Because the weapon belonged to Victor, the killer likely did not bring a weapon with them and acted during a sudden confrontation.

Evidence File 02

Security Camera Log

The gallery’s security system shows the hallway cameras outside Victor’s office went offline between 8:55 PM and 9:05 PM.

This outage happened at the same time witnesses reported hearing an argument.

The shutdown appears to have been done manually through the security system.

Evidence File 03

Keycard Access Record

Electronic keycard records show that Tyler Reed’s security card was used to enter the hallway leading to Victor’s office during the camera outage.

This places him near the office at the most critical time.

No other staff keycards were recorded in that area during those minutes.

Evidence File 04

Victor’s Phone Message

Earlier that evening, Victor sent a message that read: “We need to talk tonight about what you've been doing.”

Investigators believe the message was directed at someone he suspected of wrongdoing.

The message suggests Victor planned to confront that person during the event.

Evidence File 05

Storage Room Inventory Report

A recent inventory check showed that several valuable paintings were missing from the gallery storage room.

Victor had begun reviewing security logs to determine who had access to the room.

This discovery may have led to the confrontation that occurred in his office.

Evidence File 06

Fingerprints on the Desk

Fingerprints recovered from Victor’s desk include several belonging to Tyler Reed, located near the letter opener.

While it is normal for staff to enter the office occasionally, the placement of the prints suggests he may have been near the weapon shortly before the attack.
